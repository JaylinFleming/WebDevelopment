TIMELINE

Early 3D Acceleration (1990s)

1995

3dfx Voodoo Graphics

First big consumer 3D accelerator; popularized dedicated add-in boards and Glide API.

1998

NVIDIA RIVA TNT2

Improved multi-texturing and fill-rate; set the stage for the GeForce line.

1999

NVIDIA GeForce 256

Coined the term “GPU” and added hardware T&L, reshaping real-time 3D rendering.

Programmable Shaders (2000s)

2002

ATI Radeon 9700 Pro

DX9 pioneer with advanced shader models; major image quality & performance jump.

2006

GeForce 8800 GTX

First widely adopted unified shader architecture; huge step for flexibility and performance.

2006

CUDA

Enabled general-purpose compute on GPUs, paving the way for acceleration in AI and HPC.

GPU & Efficiency (2010s)

2012

AMD GCN

Graphics Core Next architecture optimized for parallel compute and modern APIs.

2014

NVIDIA Maxwell

Architectural focus on efficiency; set the tone for cooler, quieter high-end GPUs.

2016

DL Boom

GPU-accelerated deep learning gains momentum with cuDNN, TensorFlow, and PyTorch.

Ray Tracing & AI Era (2020s)

2018–2020

RTX & Hardware RT

Dedicated ray-tracing hardware and AI upscaling (DLSS) redefine real-time graphics.

2020–2023

AMD RDNA / RDNA 2/3

New architectures with improved efficiency and RT support; strong console lineage.

2020s

AI Acceleration

Tensor and AI cores become standard; GPUs drive LLMs, vision, and generative tools.

Next

What’s Coming

Next-generation GPUs will utilize modular chiplet architectures, ultra-high-bandwidth stacked memory, and tightly integrated hardware-software designs to provide smarter, faster and more power efficient performance for AI and data-intensive workloads.
