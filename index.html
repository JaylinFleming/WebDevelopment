Introduction

A Graphics Processing Unit (GPU) is a specialized electronic circuit designed to accelerate the processing of images and videos for display on a computer screen. Unlike a Central Processing Unit (CPU), which is optimized for general-purpose computing tasks, a GPU is specifically engineered to handle the complex mathematical calculations required for rendering graphics.
